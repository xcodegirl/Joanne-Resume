- NST: Network Security Technician

- CYBR: Cyber and Security Professional
- FSD: Full Stack Web Developer

## Tech Certifications

**Q: When should students complete their tech certification exams?**
A: After they have completed all relevant coursework. Students have up to 18 months after their graduation date to request exam vouchers and complete the exams.

**Q: Why should students complete their tech certification exams?**
A: To increase their employability. Tech certification has many benefits:
- Demonstrates commitment to professional development
- Provides a distinct edge in hiring decisions
- Validates hands-on skills and industry knowledge
- Enhances career growth and earning potential
- Meets employer and industry requirements for cybersecurity roles

## Student Readiness and Fit

**Q: In addition to developing technical/computer skills, what other skills should students expect to develop during their tech program?**
A: Analytical and problem-solving skills such as logical thinking, attention to detail and critical thinking. Soft skills such as patience, ability to support/guide others, adaptability, excellent communication and collaboration skills, continuous learning, customer service. See 10 Skills You Didn't Know Could Land You an IT Job (CompTIA, n.d.).

**FSD** *(Joanne's answer)*: Holistic view or big picture thinking. Agnosticism: understanding the underlying principles allows you to adapt and learn new languages and frameworks easily. Learning from others: engaging in the developer community through forums and open-source projects. Communication skills (not necessarily english language skills, but strong technical reading/writing skills). Become a "polyglot": learning several programming languages and learning new languages and APIs as they come out.

**Q: What type of prior knowledge is recommended for each program?**

**FSD** *(Joanne's answer)*: interest in developing web pages.

DA: Basic knowledge of data analysis concepts is recommended. Familiarity with spreadsheets (Excel/Google Sheets), databases (SQL), and data visualization tools (Tableau/Power BI) is helpful. Understanding fundamental statistics, data cleaning, and interpretation techniques will support success in the program. Critical thinking, problem-solving skills, and an eagerness to learn are essential for developing strong analytical capabilities and deriving meaningful insights from data.

CST: none

NST: Basic knowledge of computer systems, networking concepts, and the internet is recommended.

CYBR: Basic knowledge of computer systems, networking concepts, and the internet is recommended. Familiarity with operating systems (Windows/Linux), cybersecurity fundamentals, and cloud computing basics is helpful. Critical thinking, problem-solving skills, and an eagerness to learn are essential for success.

ST: No technical knowledge is required to start this Program. However, it's good to have knowledge of the software development life cycle. Analytical skills like problem solving, attention to detail, and logical reasoning will help to have a successful completion of this program.

## Practicum and Employment

**Q: What should students expect when it comes to practicum and during initial employment?**
A: Job-shadowing, limited access to company systems/info/data (due to security reasons), "grunt work", on-call duties.

**FSD** *(Joanne's answer)*: Jumping into any or all aspects of the "tech stack" of the company: front-end and/or back-end. Every company has a unique tech stack. Students will be expected to read and write code (peer review) and fit into an agile development environment with a steep learning curve. Therefore, being able to follow technical instruction will be key. There will be collaboration with several stakeholders at the company including developers, business analysts, project managers, and product owners.

**Q: What kind of jobs can I apply for when I graduate? Are there a lot of job opportunities for graduates with no work experience?**

DA: Many companies hire candidates with strong analytical skills, even without prior IT experience. While competition exists, job opportunities are available for those who build a portfolio with real-world data projects, gain certifications (Google Data Analytics, Microsoft Power BI, SQL), and seek internships or freelance work. Networking through LinkedIn, industry events, and professional groups can also improve job prospects. Critical thinking, problem-solving, and communication skills are essential to standing out in the job market. Graduates of the Data Analyst Program can apply for entry-level roles such as: Data Analyst, Business Intelligence Analyst, Marketing Analyst, Financial/Data Reporting Analyst, Operations Analyst.

CYBR: Many entry-level jobs exist, and certifications help boost employability, even without prior IT experience. Graduates can apply for roles like Cybersecurity Analyst, SOC Analyst, IT Support, Risk & Compliance Analyst.

**FSD** *(Joanne's answer)*: Working with specific cloud services and APIs, front-end technologies (eg. javascript), back-end technologies (eg. python), database management systems, CI/CD pipelines, webmaster (eg. wordpress). Part of a collaborative, agile team. Graduates without work experience should have an active portfolio and keep up to date on new paradigms and frameworks.

**Q: What can a graduate from each program do for a prospective employer or practicum site (in plain language)?**

**FSD** *(Joanne's answer)*: A full-stack web developer is someone who builds entire web applications from the ground up, handling both the parts users see (the front-end) and the parts that run in the background (the back-end).
- Build complete web applications: They create both the user-facing parts of the website (like buttons, forms, etc.) and the behind-the-scenes functions (like databases and servers) that make it all work.
- Make sure the website is easy to use and looks good, improving the user's experience (UI/UX best practices).
- Create mobile-first, responsive designs: They ensure the website works well on all devices, including phones and tablets, adjusting the layout so it looks good on any screen size.
- Optimize queries and ensure data security: They make sure the website runs efficiently and that user data is kept safe from hackers.
- They ensure the app can handle more users over time and is protected from potential security threats.
- Contribute to AI/ML integrations: They might add AI or machine learning features to the app, like personalized recommendations or smart chatbots.

DA: An entry-level Data Analyst graduate can collect, clean, and analyze data, create reports and dashboards, identify trends, and support decision-making. They can assist with organizing business data, improving efficiency, and providing insights using tools like Excel, SQL, and Power BI.

CYBR: An entry-level cybersecurity graduate can identify risks, set up protections, monitor threats, assist with audits, update software, support IT teams, and educate employees.

ST: An entry-level software tester will run predefined manual test cases, identify and log defects in a bug tracking tool (like Jira), provide clear reproduction steps, review user stories and requirements, re-test after changes, participate in daily standups and QA meetings, and assist seniors in writing test cases and bug reports.
