## AI Expertise

### What I do with AI (daily practice)
- Generative AI for content development: drafting course materials, case studies, exercises, and examples, always with accuracy review and editorial refinement
- Prompt engineering for structured output: system prompts, iterative refinement, chain-of-thought elicitation, output format control
- AI-assisted coding with GitHub Copilot, Claude, and ChatGPT: scaffolding, debugging, refactoring, and code review
- CLI and workflow automation: using AI to interface with Azure, GitHub, and shell tooling
- Multi-model practice: Claude, ChatGPT, GitHub Copilot, DeepSeek, Recraft AI, with different tools for different jobs

### What I teach about AI
- Responsible AI use as professional practice: students are ultimately responsible for every line of code they produce, with or without AI
- A three-level AI assessment framework (adapted from Perkins et al., 2024), from no-AI assessments through to AI-assisted task completion with mandatory human evaluation
- Citation of AI-generated code: inline comments, README documentation, and disclosure statements, with real code examples
- Terms of service, copyright, and institutional policy (Steam, NASA, GPL, APA guidelines) applied to software contexts
- Bias awareness and hallucination detection: AI output is a first draft requiring editorial judgment

### AI and assessment design
*From Robertson's academic integrity training and the Academic Integrity Inter-Institutional Meeting (AIIIM 2026):*
- "Authentic" learning and assessment: assignments grounded in real, local contexts, where generic AI output cannot succeed without genuine human judgment
- Scaffolded assignments with checkpoints: milestone grading and visible work in progress rather than a single final deliverable
- Developing professional judgment of AI output: students audit, correct, and debug flawed, biased, or hallucinated AI drafts
- Peer review and peer grading of AI-assisted work against concrete rubrics
